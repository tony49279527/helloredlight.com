Why B2B Pricing Is Different from Retail

Before we get into specific numbers, it's worth understanding why wholesale pricing works the way it does in this industry. I've seen buyers compare factory-direct quotes to Amazon retail prices and get confused. Here's what's different:

Retail prices include — branding costs, Amazon fees (15–30%), warehousing, advertising (up to 25% of COGS), and profit margin for at least one middleman. A panel that retails for $499 on Amazon might cost $179 at wholesale.

Wholesale prices cover — materials, labor, compliance testing (FDA, CE, RoHS), packaging, and the factory's margin. That's it. You're paying for the product, not the marketing machine around it.

The trap a lot of new distributors fall into is comparing wholesale quotes from Chinese factories against retail prices from established US brands. That's apples to oranges. A legitimate wholesale price should give you room for 2x to 4x retail markup while still being competitive.

The "Too Cheap" Trap: What $35 Panels Actually Cost You

I see new buyers get burned by this all the time. A quick search on Alibaba shows red light therapy panels for as little as $35. The unit economics look amazing until you factor in the hidden costs.

Independent testing by GembaRed in 2023 found that 73% of budget panels failed to meet their advertised irradiance specifications.[2] A panel claiming 500 mW/cm² delivering 50 mW/cm² isn't a bargain — it's a liability. Your clients won't see results. They'll ask for refunds. And in the B2B space, that reputation damage is expensive.

Real therapeutic irradiance at a treatment distance of 6 inches should be 100–150 mW/cm². Anything below that and you're essentially selling a glow stick. Legitimate manufacturers are transparent about their third-party test results. If a supplier can't show you independent irradiance data, walk away.

Additional Costs to Budget For

The equipment itself is just one piece of the puzzle. Here's what else you need to factor in:

  • Shipping & customs — Sea freight from China to US West Coast: roughly $2,000–$5,000 per 20ft container. Air freight is faster but 3–5x more expensive.
  • Electrical setup — Commercial beds need 220V circuits. Installation runs $500–$4,000 depending on your existing infrastructure.
  • Warranty reserves — Budget 2–3% of wholesale cost for warranty claims. A 2-year warranty is standard from reputable suppliers.
  • Certification transfers — If you're private labeling, some suppliers charge a fee to transfer FDA registration or CE certificates to your brand.
  • Samples — Always, always order samples before your first bulk order. Budget $100–$500 for sample shipping and testing.

5 Questions to Ask Every Supplier Before You Buy

I've been on both sides of these conversations. Here's what I'd ask if I were buying today:

  1. "Can you show me third-party irradiance test results?" — If they hesitate or send marketing material instead of data, move on.
  2. "What's the real MOQ, and does it vary by product?" — Some suppliers quote low MOQ for standard panels but 500+ for masks. Get it in writing.
  3. "What certifications do you hold, and can I see the certificates?" — FDA registration numbers can be verified on the FDA website. CE marks should specify the notified body number.
  4. "What's your warranty policy, and what's excluded?" — The standard is 2 years on LEDs, 1 year on power supply. Anything less is a red flag.
  5. "Can I visit the factory, or do a video call walkthrough?" — Not everyone can travel to China, but a supplier who refuses a video walkthrough of their production line is hiding something.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum budget to start a red light therapy business?

For a basic setup with 2 mini-panels and 1 full-body panel, budget $2,500–$3,500 for equipment. With shipping and setup costs, plan for $5,000–$8,000 total to open a treatment room.

Is it cheaper to buy direct from China?

Yes, factory-direct pricing is 40–60% lower than buying from a US-based distributor. But you need to vet the supplier thoroughly. The money you save is worthless if the quality isn't there.

How much should I mark up red light therapy equipment?

Standard B2B markup is 2–4x wholesale cost. A $179 panel wholesales to your customers at $350–$500. A $5,000 bed wholesales at $10,000–$15,000. For retail (Amazon, e-commerce), markup is typically 3–5x.

Do I need FDA clearance to resell red light therapy devices?

In the US, the FDA requires that devices sold for medical purposes have FDA registration or clearance. If you're selling for "wellness" or "general wellness" use, the requirements are less strict. But if you make any therapeutic claims, you need FDA clearance. Work with a supplier who understands compliance for your target market.

What's the difference between a $200 panel and a $2,000 panel?

At the risk of oversimplifying: LED quality and count. A $200 retail panel typically has 30–60 low-power LEDs and an irradiance of 30–50 mW/cm². A $2,000 panel has 200–500 dual-chip medical-grade LEDs with 100–150 mW/cm² irradiance, better cooling, and a longer lifespan. Both can be called "red light therapy panels." One actually works.[2]

How much does a commercial red light therapy bed cost?

B2B wholesale pricing for commercial beds starts at $4,999 for entry-level models and goes up to $85,000 for flagship medical-grade systems. Most wellness centers invest between $5,000 and $15,000 per bed for a reliable, high-utilization unit.

Can I get samples before placing a bulk order?

Absolutely — and you should. Reputable manufacturers offer samples at a reasonable cost (typically $100–$500 depending on the product), and many deduct the sample cost from your first bulk order. If a supplier pressures you to place a bulk order without sampling first, treat that as a red flag.

What payment terms do Chinese manufacturers typically require?

The industry standard is 30% deposit upfront, 70% balance before shipment. T/T (wire transfer) is the most common payment method. Be cautious with suppliers demanding 100% upfront payment — it's unusual and risky.
