1. How to Choose the Right Wavelengths

A factory visit will quickly teach you that not all red light is equal. The wavelength — measured in nanometers (nm) — determines how deep the light penetrates into tissue, and therefore what conditions it can treat. Choosing the wrong wavelength for your target market means your customers won't see results, and they'll blame your brand.

Wavelength Color Penetration Depth Best For Typical User
630nmBright RedEpidermis (skin surface)Acne, wrinkles, skin texture, collagen productionBeauty salons, aesthetic clinics
660nmDeep RedDermis (mid-layer)Inflammation reduction, wound healing, mitochondrial functionWellness clinics, dermatologists
850nmNear-Infrared (invisible)Muscle, joint, boneDeep tissue recovery, joint pain, muscle repairGyms, physiotherapy, sports medicine

Factory Insight

The most common mistake we see from first-time buyers is ordering single-wavelength panels (e.g., only 660nm) to save cost. Six months later they come back asking for dual or triple-wavelength panels because their clients need both skin and deep tissue treatment. If your budget allows, start with at least dual-wavelength (660nm + 850nm). The cost difference at wholesale volume is typically under 15%, but the addressable market doubles.

2. Which Certifications Actually Matter

Every manufacturer's website lists certifications. But not all certifications carry equal weight, and some are requirements — not differentiators. Here is what each one actually means for your business.

FDA 510(k) Clearance

Required for US market. Confirms the device is substantially equivalent to an existing legally marketed device. Without this, you cannot legally sell as a medical device in the US. Verify the clearance number — any legitimate manufacturer will provide it on request.

Medical CE (Class IIa)

Required for EU/EEA. Indicates conformity with European medical device regulations. Class IIa covers therapeutic devices. Ensure the CE certificate specifically states "medical" — general CE (for electronics) is not the same.

ISO 13485

Quality management system specific to medical device manufacturing. This is the factory-level certification that shows the production process — not just the product — meets international standards. Important for distributors and large buyers.

RoHS / FCC

RoHS certifies no hazardous substances in electronics. FCC covers electromagnetic compatibility. These are basic compliance requirements, not quality indicators. Every legitimate manufacturer should have them.

⚠ Red Flag

If a manufacturer cannot show you the actual certificate documents (not just logos on a website), walk away. Certification fraud in this industry is real — we have seen factories display certification marks they never obtained. Always request the PDF copies.

3. Understanding Power Density and Irradiance

Irradiance — measured in mW/cm² — tells you how much light energy reaches the treatment area. This is the single most important technical specification, and also the most frequently misrepresented.

When comparing manufacturers, always ask for irradiance measurements at a specific distance from the panel surface. A reading taken at 0 inches is meaningless for real-world use, where patients are typically 6–12 inches away. We measure irradiance at 6 inches (15cm) as standard. At this distance, a well-engineered high-power panel should deliver 80–120+ mW/cm² in the 660nm and 850nm bands.

Factory Insight

Some manufacturers inflate irradiance numbers by using solar power meters (which measure across the full spectrum) instead of spectroradiometers (which measure at specific wavelengths). A solar meter reading of 200 mW/cm² tells you nothing about therapeutic output. Ask: "What instrument do you use to measure irradiance, and at which wavelengths?" If they cannot answer this question, find another supplier.

4. What to Ask When Doing OEM/ODM

Private labeling is the fastest path to building your own red light therapy brand. But OEM manufacturing has variables that affect both your unit cost and your time to market.

Custom Wavelength Spectrum

Can the factory adjust the LED ratio? For example, a clinic-focused brand might want 70% 660nm / 30% 850nm, while a gym brand might want the opposite. This requires in-house LED sourcing and PCB design capability — not every factory can do it.

Shell Color & Logo Engraving

Standard options include white, black, and custom Pantone colors. Logo engraving or silk-screening on the panel frame typically adds 3–5 days to production. MOQ for fully custom shells is usually higher than for standard colors.

Branded Packaging

Custom box design, manual, and inserts. Lead time for first-run packaging is typically 15–25 days after design approval. Reorders are faster once templates are established.

Timeline

From signed agreement to first shipment, a typical OEM project takes 30–60 days for the first order, including sampling, adjustments, and production. Rush orders are possible but cost more.

Factory Insight

The biggest hidden cost is not the unit price — it is shipping and import duties. A single 1500W panel weighs 15–20 kg. At volume, sea freight is economical but slow (25–35 days to US/EU ports). Air freight takes 5–7 days but can cost 3–5x more. We recommend first-time buyers do a small air-freight order for market testing, then switch to sea freight once demand is proven.

6. Warranty and After-Sales: What Good Support Looks Like

Red light therapy panels are electronic devices. Components will eventually fail. The difference between a supplier who supports your brand and one who disappears after the sale comes down to the warranty terms and support structure.

✔ Look For

  • 3-year warranty minimum (1 year is too short for commercial use)
  • Written warranty terms, not verbal promises
  • Replacement parts inventory that ships within 48 hours
  • Technical support available in English during your business hours
  • Lifetime technical support commitment

✘ Red Flags

  • Warranty that requires you to ship defective units back to China at your cost
  • No English-speaking support contact
  • "We never have defects" — every factory has defects. The honest ones tell you their defect rate.
  • No spare parts program
